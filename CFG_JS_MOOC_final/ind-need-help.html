Tips to save money on your grocery shopping
  • Get low priced ingredients by going to the discount section of a supermarket in the early morning or after 6pm and look for the yellow discount stickers. Top tip: Many stores, for example, Tesco, will have a dedicated shelf or freezer area for discounted items, so find out where that is in your local shop to save you time and money.

  • If shopping for groceries online, look out for flash sales at Morrisons and Ocado. Top tip: You will find the flash sales just before you checkout highlighted in red font.

  • Try supermarket own brand products. Some are so good that it is hard to tell the difference from branded products. Top tip: replace Heinz baked beans with own brand, we like Morrisons Baked Beans.

  • If certain branded products are difficult to replace like Heinz Tomato Ketchup, wait for the sales, buy in bulk and in bigger sizes if the product has a long shelf life or you/your family/flatmates will get through it before expiry. Top tip: we’re loving Gold Star Tomato Ketchup 690g for £1 from Iceland.

  • To compare the price of items you regularly buy and family favourites across various supermarkets, check Trolley  to see which shop will give you the best value.

  • Sign up to supermarket newsletters and receive offers / promos by email, post and text. Some supermarkets will send money off vouchers to your door like Lidl, so sign up to get the marketing mail, ask instore. Many supermarkets will send you voucher codes direct to your email. If you are a new customer, sign up for a welcome bonus on your first shop and some run for the first couple of shops. Iceland will send you voucher codes by text and email. Top tip: We’re no fan of junk mail but most supermarkets will send you actual offers so sign up to save money on your weekly shop!

  • Join hotukdeals and subscribe to alerts for ‘groceries’. You can also download the free app to alert direct to your mobile phone. This site/app will alert you when there is a good deal for groceries at various supermarkets as well as voucher codes that will save you money. Top tip: You can also subscribe to alerts for your favourite supermarkets so you will know a deal is available from a store you shop with, see hotukdeals

  • Save VoucherCodes   into your favourites. This site has voucher codes for many supermarkets. Sign up to get alerts to your email or download the app to get them on your phone. Top tip: Recent voucher alerts have been as much as £10 in savings for click and collect orders at Tesco.

  • Get the PayPal Honey browser extension or add-on for most web browsers that can save you money by automatically searching for voucher codes at the checkout screen or voucher/promo code box of a website. Check out their website to see how it works: Honey  Top tip: You can also get the app from your app store.

Batch cooking using a slow cooker
  • A slow cooker is about £15-20 to purchase. Think of it as an investment to make cooking easier and cheaper. Top tip: You can get the 1.8l slow cooker for £15 from Argos  or if you prefer Amazon

  • Not only will you save money on food, you will also save on gas and electricity. It’s convenient you can cook one or two times a week and store the rest of the batch you’ve made in the freezer for later in the week. You can make healthy nutritious meals using a slow cooker.

  • Plan your meals in advance so you can get the required ingredients and decide your meals for each day.

  • Do a google search for meal ideas with a slow cooker and you will find recipes as well as step by step YouTube videos. Top tip: we love these recipes for batch cooking
Food banks
  • Food banks are for those in need of help to feed themselves and their families. These emergency food parcels may include enough nutritionally balanced food to last for at least three days. These parcels can also include other household essentials like baby supplies, toiletries and cleaning supplies. To get access to a food bank, you will need to get a food bank voucher.

  • If you are in need and would like to access a food bank, you will need a referral from a local agency or organisation, for example, local Citizens Advice Bureau (CAB), your GP, job centre, welfare assistance, children's centre, housing association, social worker, local charities and support organisations. Check this website for further information: See Citizens Advice Food Bank advice

  • If you are eligible, you will receive a food bank referral voucher, which you can then use to access help by a food bank.

  • There are some food banks where no referral is required. If you are in need, you can simply turn up and receive free food. Contact your local food bank to see if you need a referral: Trussel Trust Food banks

  • There are also a number of independent community group food banks, you can find them here: independent food banks
